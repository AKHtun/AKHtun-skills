# ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — Standard Overview

## What It Is

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the world's first international standard for AI management systems. Published December 2023 by ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42. It provides a structured framework for organisations to govern AI responsibly — covering the full system lifecycle from design through decommissioning.

## Structure (10 Clauses)

| Clause | Topic | What It Requires |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | Scope | Defines what the standard covers |
| 2 | Normative References | Other standards this depends on (ISO 9000, ISO 31000, etc.) |
| 3 | Terms & Definitions | 38 defined terms (AI system, stakeholder, impact, etc.) |
| 4 | Context of the Organisation | Understand your org, stakeholders, and the AI landscape |
| 5 | Leadership | Top management commitment, AI policy, roles |
| 6 | Planning | Risk assessment, impact assessment, objectives |
| 7 | Support | Resources, competence, awareness, communication, documentation |
| 8 | Operation | Lifecycle: design → development → deploy → operate → retire |
| 9 | Performance Evaluation | Monitoring, audit, management review |
| 10 | Improvement | Nonconformity handling, corrective action, continual improvement |

## Annex A — The 38 Controls

The operational heart. 38 controls across 9 categories:

| Category | Controls | Topics |
|----------|----------|--------|
| A.2 | Policies | AI policy, alignment, review |
| A.3 | Internal Organisation | Roles, reporting concerns |
| A.4 | Resources | Data, tools, compute, people |
| A.5 | Impact Assessment | Process, documentation, fairness, societal |
| A.6 | Lifecycle | Design, dev, verify, deploy, operate, log, doc |
| A.7 | Data | Acquisition, quality, provenance, preparation |
| A.8 | Information | User docs, external reporting, incidents |
| A.9 | Use | Responsible use processes, intended use |
| A.10 | Third Parties | Suppliers, customers, responsibilities |

## Annex B — Implementation Guidance

Explains *how* to implement each clause. Includes:
- B.5.4: Individual impact assessment (fairness, transparency, privacy, safety)
- B.5.5: Societal impact assessment (environment, economy, politics, culture)
- B.5.6: Risk assessment methodology

## Why This Matters

- **Regulatory readiness**: EU AI Act, US Executive Orders, and other jurisdictions increasingly require AI governance. ISO 42001 is the voluntary-standard path to compliance.
- **Client confidence**: Enterprise and government clients increasingly ask "how do you manage AI?" before signing contracts.
- **Insurance**: Some cyber/AI liability insurers now factor governance frameworks into premiums.
- **Internal discipline**: Forces you to document what your AI actually does — which catches gaps you didn't know you had.

## What Conformity Looks Like

You don't need certification (though you can get it). Conformity means:

1. You have a documented AI policy
2. You've assessed yourself against the 38 controls
3. You have an impact assessment on file
4. You audit regularly and close gaps
5. Management reviews the system periodically

This skill implements all five.
